Getting Started Guide

Welcome to Solace Browser! This guide walks you through connecting your accounts and running your first automation. Everything happens with your approval — nothing runs without you saying yes.

1 Sign In to Solace

  • Open Solace Browser — the start page loads automatically
  • Click Continue with Google (or GitHub/Email)
  • Complete the sign-in popup — this creates your Solace account
  • You'll see the "Connected to Solace" card with your credits and plan
Tip: Your API key is stored locally on your device, encrypted. We never see your passwords. You can revoke access anytime from Settings.

2 Browse the App Store

  • Click Open App Store from the home screen
  • Browse available automations — each app shows what it does and what permissions it needs
  • Click an app to see its full details, steps, and approval flow

3 Link an Account (e.g. LinkedIn)

When you run an app for the first time, Solace Browser will help you sign in to the target service. Here's how it works with LinkedIn:

  • Click the LinkedIn Helper app in the App Store
  • Click Run Now — Solace opens LinkedIn's login page in the browser
  • You'll see LinkedIn's real login page. Enter your credentials directly — Solace watches but never stores your password
  • If LinkedIn asks for 2FA, complete it normally
  • Once logged in, Solace saves a session cookie (not your password) so future runs don't need re-login
  • The app starts its automation — you approve each action before it executes
How is this secure? Solace uses the same browser session model as if you opened Chrome yourself. Your credentials go directly to LinkedIn — Solace only sees the resulting session cookie. You can clear sessions anytime from Settings.

4 Run Your First App

Let's walk through Gmail Inbox Triage as an example:

  • Open App Store → click Gmail Inbox Triage
  • Click Run Now — the browser navigates to Gmail
  • If not logged in, sign in to Gmail (same process as LinkedIn above)
  • The app reads your inbox and generates a preview of proposed actions
  • Review the preview: "Archive 12 newsletters, flag 3 urgent items"
  • Click Approve to execute, or Decline to cancel
  • Watch each action execute in real-time — screenshots captured as evidence
Nothing happens without approval. If you close the browser or navigate away, all pending actions are cancelled (fail-closed). There is no auto-approve. You are always in control.

5 Daily Tech Trends with LinkedIn

Want to stay on top of technology trends? Here's how to set it up:

  • Install the LinkedIn Helper or Tech Trends Daily app
  • Run it once to link your LinkedIn account (see Step 3 above)
  • Go to Schedule (from the sidebar) and set it to run daily
  • Each morning, the app scans your LinkedIn feed, Hacker News, and tech blogs
  • You get a digest of top trends — approve which ones to save or share
Scheduled runs still require approval on first run. After the first approval, the app will use the same session and follow the same scopes. Budget limits apply (e.g., max 50 page visits per day).

6 Send Us Feedback

  • Click the Send Feedback button on the home screen
  • Select a category: Bug, Confusing, Feature Request, or Love It
  • Write a brief description of your experience
  • Click Send — your feedback goes directly to our team
Your feedback drives real changes. We read every submission and use it to improve Solace Browser daily. The more specific you are, the faster we can fix it.
