Glossary

Plain-language definitions for every term in Solace Browser. No jargon left behind.

Recipe
A saved automation you can replay. Recipes record what the AI did so it can repeat the same task without calling an LLM again. This makes repeated tasks nearly free.
OAuth3
How you give permission to apps. OAuth3 lets you control exactly what an AI agent can do on your behalf, for how long, and revoke access at any time.
Evidence Chain
A tamper-evident log of every action the AI takes. Each entry is hash-linked to the previous one, so you can always verify what happened, when, and in what order.
Twin
A browser session running in the cloud on your behalf. Your cloud twin can execute recipes even when your computer is off, using the permissions you delegated via OAuth3.
App Store
A curated catalog of automations you can install and run. Each app shows what it does, what permissions it needs, and requires your approval before executing any action.
Vault
A local, encrypted store for your API keys and session tokens. Your credentials stay on your device, protected with AES-256-GCM encryption. Solace never sees your passwords.
Approval Gate
A checkpoint where the AI pauses and asks for your permission before doing something. Nothing runs without your explicit yes. If you walk away, pending actions are cancelled.
Budget Gate
A spending limit you set for AI operations. If a task would exceed your budget, the system stops and asks for approval. This prevents runaway costs.
YinYang
The AI assistant inside Solace Browser. YinYang helps you navigate, answers questions, and coordinates automations. It always asks before acting.
BYOK (Bring Your Own Key)
Use your own AI API key from Anthropic, OpenAI, or other providers. We never mark up the cost and your key stays on your device.
Capsule
A self-contained app package that bundles code, data, and permissions into one portable unit. Capsules can be shared, versioned, and replayed deterministically.
Northstar
The guiding direction for the entire platform. The Northstar ensures every feature, every decision, and every automation serves one purpose: keeping you in control of your AI.
Preview
The step where YinYang shows you what it plans to do before doing it. The LLM is called once to generate a preview, you approve or decline, and only then does execution happen. This makes repeated runs nearly free.
Scope
A specific permission granted to an app via OAuth3. Scopes define exactly what an agent can do — for example, read your inbox but not send emails. Narrower scopes mean less risk.
Rung
A trust level in the verification ladder. Higher rungs unlock more capabilities but require more evidence of correct behavior. Apps start at the lowest rung and earn trust over time.
